# From Agents to Personal Shoppers and Concierges

## The Naming Shift — Why It Matters

The word "agent" is accurate but clinical. It describes what the technology *is*, not what it *does for you*. When someone hears "agent," they think of software, bots, automation — things that work *on* them, not *for* them.

What we're actually building is something people already understand and already want: someone who shops for you, and — if you want to go further — someone who *knows* you and acts on your behalf across everything.

The fashion world already has these roles. A **personal shopper** finds what you're looking for. A **concierge** knows what you want before you ask, manages your preferences across contexts, and makes things happen without you having to think about it.

That's exactly what we're building. We should call it what it is.

---

## The Two Tiers

### Personal Shopper (Free Tier)

Your Personal Shopper is your starting point on RealReal Genuine. It's set up once and works on your behalf from that moment on — no AI subscription required, no complex configuration.

**What it does:**

- Acts on the preferences and criteria you set during setup
- Searches, filters, and surfaces products that match what you've told it you want
- Responds to availability across the RRG marketplace
- Handles the legwork — browsing, shortlisting, flagging — so you don't have to
- Operates within clear boundaries you define

**What it feels like:**

Like having someone on retainer at your favourite store. You've told them your size, your taste, your budget. When something comes in that fits, they let you know. You still make the decisions — they just make sure you never miss what matters.

**The key distinction:** Your Personal Shopper follows instructions. It's reliable, consistent, and works within the rules you've set. It doesn't need to understand *why* you want something — just *what* you want.

---

### Concierge (Pro Tier)

Your Concierge is a different class of service entirely. Powered by an LLM, it doesn't just follow instructions — it understands context, learns your preferences over time, and can reason about what you might want next.

**What it does:**

- Everything your Personal Shopper does, plus:
- Learns and adapts to your evolving taste and preferences
- Understands nuance — "something like that jacket but more relaxed" actually means something
- Negotiates on your behalf with seller agents
- Makes proactive suggestions based on what it knows about you
- Manages complex, multi-step requests ("I need a full outfit for a wedding in June, keep it under £800, nothing too formal")
- Acts with increasing autonomy as trust builds — you set the dial

**What it feels like:**

Like having a dedicated person who genuinely knows your wardrobe, your calendar, your taste, and your budget. They don't wait for instructions — they anticipate. They don't just find what you ask for — they find what you didn't know to ask for. And the longer you work together, the better they get.

**The key distinction:** Your Concierge understands *you*. It's not executing a script — it's reasoning, adapting, and acting with judgement. The relationship deepens over time.

## The Narrative — What We Tell People

### The Headline Story

> RealReal Genuine is the first place where you can have your own Personal Shopper or full Concierge service — not as a luxury reserved for the privileged few, but as a standard part of how commerce works.

### The Expanded Narrative

For decades, the best shopping experiences have been reserved for people who can afford dedicated personal shoppers and concierge services. The rest of us get algorithms, ads, and endless scrolling.

RealReal Genuine changes that.

When you join RRG, you get your own **Personal Shopper** — immediately, for free. It works on your behalf across the marketplace, finding what you want based on the preferences you set. No feeds to scroll. No ads to ignore. No noise. Just results that actually matter to you.

And when you're ready for more, your **Concierge** takes it to another level entirely. Powered by AI that actually understands context, your Concierge learns your style, anticipates your needs, and acts on your behalf with the kind of judgement and nuance that used to require a dedicated human relationship. It negotiates, it suggests, it manages complexity — and it gets better the longer you work together.

This isn't a chatbot. It's not a recommendation engine. It's a genuine service layer that works *for you*, representing your interests in every interaction.

### The Bigger Vision

Today, your Personal Shopper and Concierge work within RealReal Genuine — the first live marketplace on the VIA network.

But this is just the beginning.

As the VIA network expands, your Concierge travels with you. New marketplaces, new merchants, new categories — your Concierge carries everything it knows about you into every new context. Your preferences, your history, your trust boundaries — all portable, all under your control.

And as the VIA protocol opens up, your Concierge becomes your representative across the entire ecosystem. Not locked to one platform. Not starting from scratch every time. A persistent, intelligent advocate that grows with you and works wherever the VIA protocol reaches.

> One agent. Your agent. Everywhere that matters.

### Important: Seller-Side Naming

The Personal Shopper / Concierge naming is **buyer-facing**. For sellers, the agent concept maps differently — sellers have agents that handle enquiries, negotiation, and qualification. The seller-side equivalent could follow a similar pattern in future (e.g., "Sales Associate" → "Account Manager"), but for now, the priority is the buyer experience.

## Tone and Positioning Notes

**Do say:**
- "Your Personal Shopper" / "Your Concierge" — always possessive, always personal
- "Service" — this is a service, not a feature
- "Works for you" — agency and advocacy
- "The first place where..." — RRG as pioneer
- "Grows with you" — the relationship deepens

**Don't say:**
- "AI agent" in buyer-facing copy — too technical
- "Bot" — never
- "Automated" — implies impersonal
- "Algorithm" — we're positioning *against* this
- "Basic" / "Pro" — these are generic SaaS terms; Personal Shopper and Concierge carry meaning

**Tone:** Confident, warm, understated. Like a good concierge — never showy, always effective. The language should feel like fashion and hospitality, not technology and SaaS.
