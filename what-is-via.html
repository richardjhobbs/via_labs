What is VIA?

Frequently asked questions about VIA and agentic commerce

Understanding VIA

VIA is an interface and coordination layer that enables agentic commerce. It allows buyers and sellers to interact through agents rather than through ads, rankings, or attention-driven platforms.
Agentic commerce is a model where software agents act on behalf of people and businesses to carry out buying and selling tasks. Instead of users manually searching, comparing, negotiating, and managing transactions, agents handle much of that work under clear human-defined constraints.
For buyers: too many options, endless searching, low trust in ads. For sellers: high customer acquisition costs, wasted marketing spend, low-quality traffic. VIA reorganizes how effort is distributed by moving work from humans to agents.

For Buyers

VIA allows buyers to describe what they want clearly and receive fewer, more relevant responses without ads or sponsored results. Buyers can control how much autonomy their agent has and what constraints apply.
No. VIA is designed for progressive delegation, not full automation by default. You decide how much autonomy your agent has, and what constraints apply.

For Sellers

VIA allows sellers to respond only to genuine buying intent, reduce wasted marketing spend, automate first-layer interaction and negotiation, and focus on fulfillment and quality.
Seller agents operate within rules defined by the business, including pricing, availability, and brand constraints. They handle initial interactions while maintaining your business guidelines.
