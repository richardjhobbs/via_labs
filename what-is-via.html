What is VIA?

Frequently asked questions about VIA and agentic commerce

Understanding VIA

VIA is an interface and coordination layer that enables agentic commerce. It allows buyers and sellers to interact through agents rather than through ads, rankings, or attention-driven platforms.
Agentic commerce is a model where software agents act on behalf of people and businesses to carry out buying and selling tasks.
For buyers: too many options, endless searching, low trust in ads. For sellers: high customer acquisition costs, wasted marketing spend, low-quality traffic.

For Buyers

VIA allows buyers to describe what they want clearly and receive fewer, more relevant responses without ads or sponsored results.
No. VIA is designed for progressive delegation, not uncontrolled automation. You decide how much freedom your agent has.

For Sellers

VIA allows sellers to respond only to genuine buying intent and automate first-layer interaction and negotiation.
Your agent handles enquiries, qualification, availability checks, and negotiation within rules you define.
